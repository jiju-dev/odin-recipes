NORWEGIAN RHUBARB AND ALMOND CAKE

DESCRIPTION

This delicate Norwegian Rhubarb and Almond Cake offers a unique blend of flavors, combining tart rhubarb with the richness of almond. Perfect for a spring brunch, this delightful breakfast cake originates from Norway, where rhubarb flourishes in the cool climate. Rhubarb, known for its perennial and abundant growth, emerges as one of the earliest crops of spring, earning its place as a beloved ingredient wherever it is found. While prevalent across the northern half of the US, rhubarb can be readily sourced from your local produce aisle. Its tangy, berry-like taste pairs exquisitely with almond, creating a delectable coffee cake that perfectly complements the season.

INGREDIENTS

  • 10 Tbsp unsalted butter at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs at room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 cup whole milk or half and half
  • 1 1/2 tsp almond extract
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped rhubarb or about 3-5 stalks, depending on size.
  • 3 Tbsp raw sugar
  • 3 Tbsp sliced almonds

STEPS

  1. Preheat your oven to 350F
  2. Lightly grease an 9 inch springform pan and place a round of parchment paper at the bottom.
  3. Cream the butter and sugar in the bowl of a stand mixer or with electric mixers until light and fluffy. This will take a couple of minutes. Donâ€™t skip this step, the creaming incorporates air into the batter that helps the cake bake up light and fluffy.
  4. Add in the eggs one at a time, beating the mixture between each addition until they are fully incorporated.
  5. Add the flour, baking powder, and then the milk or half and half, along with the almond extract. Mix until combined.
  6. Spread the batter into your prepared pan, making sure it is relatively even.
  7. Scatter the chopped rhubarb over the top of the cake, pressing it in just a little bit.
  8. Scatter the raw sugar and sliced almonds over the cake
  9. Bake for about 30-40 minutes, until set in the middle, and golden brown on top. Let the cake cool for about 10 minutes before carefully unlatching the pan.

NOTES

  • Buttermilk can be used instead of milk.
  • Try raspberries or blueberries in place of the rhubarb.
  • Substitute vanilla extract for the almond extract
